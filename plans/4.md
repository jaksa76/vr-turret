# Plan: Issue 4 — Sun

## Summary

Add a visible sun sphere to the scene that matches the existing directional light in direction and color. The directional light sits at position `-5 0.1 2`, which means it illuminates the scene from that direction. The sun should appear as a self-illuminated sphere placed far away along that same directional vector so the light and the visual source are coherent.

## Implementation Steps

### 1. Compute sun position from the directional light

The directional light is at `position="-5 0.1 2"`. In A-Frame, a directional light shines FROM its position toward the origin, so the sun should appear in the direction of that position vector, pushed far away so it sits on the skybox.

Normalize `(-5, 0.1, 2)`:
- magnitude ≈ 5.39
- normalized ≈ `(-0.928, 0.019, 0.371)`
- scaled by 500 units ≈ `(-464, 9, 185)` → use `"-460 10 185"` (rounded)

### 2. Add a sun sphere to `index.html`

Place the sun entity directly after the directional light (line 58) so the two are visually grouped:

```html
<!-- Sun — position matches directional light direction, pushed to skybox distance -->
<a-sphere position="-460 10 185" radius="18"
    material="color: #FFF; emissive: #FFF8E7; emissiveIntensity: 1; shader: flat">
</a-sphere>
```

Key material choices:
- `shader: flat` — disables A-Frame's default Phong shading so the sphere is uniformly bright regardless of incoming light.
- `color: #FFF` — matches the directional light color exactly.
- `emissive: #FFF8E7` — very slight warm tint for the glow (near-white, not distracting).
- `emissiveIntensity: 1` — fully self-lit; no dependence on scene lighting.
- No `ammo-body` or physics needed; the sun is purely decorative.
- No `colliding` class; raycasts should pass through it.

`radius="18"` at distance ~470 units subtends roughly 2.2 degrees of arc — visually comparable to a bright sun near the horizon.

## Risks / Edge Cases

1. **Z-fighting with the sky sphere** — The sun is placed at ~470 units while the `a-sky` sphere has a default radius of 5000 units. At 470 units the sun is well inside the sky sphere, so no z-fighting is expected.

2. **Fog or background mismatch** — There is no fog in the scene; the nebula sky is a texture on a sphere. Because the sun is placed inside that sphere its sphere geometry will render on top of the nebula image, which is the intended effect.

3. **Sun visible through the platform** — The battleship model is at `y: -5.4` below the camera. If the sun's Y position is above the horizon the platform will correctly occlude it when looking down. At `y: 10` the sun sits just above the horizon and should remain above the deck in normal gameplay views.

4. **Flat shader availability** — A-Frame's built-in `flat` shader is always present; no CDN dependency required.

5. **Performance** — A single static sphere with a flat shader has negligible render cost.
